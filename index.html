How To Prepare For An Interview

Prep time: 1-2 hours
Helpfulness rating: 100%

Interviews for articles or feature stories can be intimidating for first-timers. Being prepared is the best way to get the most out of any interview. Follow these steps to look like a pro!

  • What you'll need

  • Reporter's notepad
  • Recorder
  • Contact information
  • General idea of what you're writing about

  1. Know the point of your story.

    Before crafting questions or scheduling an interview, make sure you have at least a sense of what you are writing about and why this person is important to what you are doing.

  2. Research your person-of-interest

    Do some preliminary research about the person you are interviewing. Look on any personal/company websites or anything written about them already. Going into the interview with a brief background will save you from spending time on basic questions.

  3. Have a plan for taking notes.

    Most journalist record interviews and transcribe them later, assuming it isn't being video-recorded, and some prefer to take notes on a regular reporter's notepad. Using a hybrid of both methods is a great way to stay engaged with the interviewee without missing any information. If you choose to record on a smartphone, make sure to set it to 'do not disturb' to avoid distractions that may interfere with the recording. And remember: always ask permission to record an interview!.

  4. Prepare questions.

    Write a handful of open-ended questions you think are important. Try to avoid asking repetitive questions. Questions will arise on their own during the interview and conversation will take off, so write in your reporter's pad the questions you really want answered.

  5. Have a meeting place planned.

    When planning somewhere to meet, try to go to your interviewee instead of the other way around. Meet them in their enviroment or somewhere that is important to them. This will make your writing feel authentic and will make your subject more comfortable. Make sure it is free of distractions at the time of your interview.

  6. Confirm with your subject.

    Always confirm with your interviewee a day or so before your interview. This will not only make you look professional but will help you catch miscommunications ahead of time. If you find yourself struggling to meet with someone, it's better to know early that it's time to pick someone else.
